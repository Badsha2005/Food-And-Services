Authentic Pad Thai

The national dish of Thailand with perfect balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors

Thai Cuisine
Intermediate
30 mins
2 servings
4.9 (287)

This authentic Pad Thai recipe comes straight from the streets of Bangkok. Made with rice noodles, shrimp, tofu, and a signature tamarind-based sauce, it's a perfect balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. The key to perfect Pad Thai lies in the preparation and high heat cooking technique.

Ingredients

For the Pad Thai:

  • 8 oz (225g) rice noodles (medium width)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup firm tofu, cubed
  • 8-10 medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • Lime wedges for serving

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp chili powder (optional)

Instructions

  1. Soak rice noodles in room temperature water for 30-40 minutes until pliable but still firm. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the sauce by mixing tamarind paste, palm sugar, fish sauce, and chili powder in a small bowl. Stir until sugar dissolves.
  3. Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add 1 tbsp oil and swirl to coat. Add garlic and stir-fry for 10 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add tofu and shrimp. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until shrimp turns pink. Push everything to one side of the wok.
  5. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the empty space and crack in the egg. Let it set slightly, then scramble and mix with other ingredients.
  6. Add drained noodles and sauce. Use two utensils to toss and stir constantly for 2-3 minutes until noodles are tender and sauce is absorbed.
  7. Add bean sprouts and green onions. Toss for another 30 seconds until sprouts are slightly wilted but still crisp.
  8. Transfer to serving plates. Garnish with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and additional bean sprouts. Serve immediately.

Special Equipment

  • Wok or large skillet (essential for proper stir-frying)
  • Mortar and pestle (for crushing peanuts)
